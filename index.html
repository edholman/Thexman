Common Questions I Answer Daily

Clear answers to help you understand your mortgage options

How much home can I actually afford? +

Your affordability depends on multiple factors: income, debts, credit score, down payment, and the loan type. I'll help you understand your true buying power and ensure your monthly payment aligns with your lifestyle and long-term goals—not just what lenders say you qualify for.

What's my first step as a first-time buyer? +

Start with a free consultation where we'll review your financial situation, discuss your goals, and get you pre-approved. Pre-approval gives you clarity on your budget and makes you a stronger buyer when you find the right home. I'll guide you through every step with patience and clear explanations.

How do VA loans really work? +

VA loans are one of the best benefits for veterans and active military. You can buy with $0 down, no PMI (mortgage insurance), and typically get better rates. I've helped hundreds of veterans navigate their benefits and will ensure you understand every advantage available to you.

Should I use an LLC for my rental property? +

It depends on your situation, goals, and risk tolerance. LLCs offer liability protection but affect how you qualify for loans. I'll walk you through the pros and cons based on your specific investment strategy and help you structure your purchase optimally.

What credit score do I need? +

Minimum scores vary by loan type (580 for FHA, 620+ for conventional, no minimum for some VA loans), but higher scores get better rates. Even if your credit needs work, I can help you understand your options and create a plan to improve your position.

How do I know if I'm getting the best rate? +

As an independent broker with access to 50+ loan products from multiple lenders, I can shop your scenario across the market. I'll show you competitive options and explain exactly what affects your rate—credit score, down payment, loan type, and current market conditions.

What's the difference between FHA and Conventional? +

FHA loans allow lower down payments (3.5%) and more flexible credit requirements, but require mortgage insurance. Conventional loans typically need higher credit scores and larger down payments but may have lower total costs long-term. I'll help you determine which makes more financial sense for your situation.

Can I buy investment property with no money down? +

While most investment properties require 15-25% down, there are creative strategies: house hacking with FHA (live in one unit, rent others), seller financing, HELOC from existing property, or partnership structures. Let's discuss your specific situation and explore your options.
